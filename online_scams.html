Section 2: Common Types of Online Scams

Online Scams

Online scams are tricks used by bad people to steal your money or personal information. Here are three common ones to watch out for:

Phishing Scams:

  • What it is: Fake emails or messages that look real, asking for your personal details like passwords or bank info.

    • Example: An email pretending to be from your bank, asking you to "verify" your account.

    Identity Theft:

  • What it is: When someone steals your personal information (like your Social Security number) to pretend to be you.

    • Example: A scammer uses your name to open a credit card without your knowledge.

    Fake Websites:

  • What it is: Websites that look real but are designed to trick you into sharing your information or money.

    • Example: A shopping site offering deals that seem too good to be true—they probably are!

    Quick Tip: Always double-check emails, messages, and websites. If something feels off, it probably is.
