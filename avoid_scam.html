Section 3: How to Recognize and Avoid Scams

Protecting Yourself from Scams

Protecting yourself from online scams is easier when you know what to look for. Here are simple tips to stay safe:

  • Check the Sender: Be cautious of emails or messages from unknown or suspicious addresses.
  • Don't Rush: Scammers often create urgency. Take your time to verify requests for personal or financial information.
  • Look for Red Flags: Poor spelling, strange links, or too-good-to-be-true offers are often signs of a scam.
  • Verify Websites: Before entering sensitive information, check the website's URL for misspellings or odd characters.
  • Use Strong Passwords: Create unique, hard-to-guess passwords for each account.
  • Enable Two-Factor Authentication (2FA): Add an extra layer of security by requiring a code sent to your phone or email to log in.

Quick Tip: When in doubt, don't click, share, or respond. Verify through a trusted source instead.
